# SaaS Customer Churn Analysis

## Project Overview
Analysis of customer churn patterns using the Telco Customer 
Churn dataset (7,032 customers after data cleaning).

## Tools Used
- Excel (data cleaning, PivotTables, combined analysis)
- Tableau (dashboard visualisation)

## Key Finding
We should launch a targeted early retention program focused on three levers — onboarding support, contract incentives, and add-on adoption — concentrated in the first 12 months of a customer's lifecycle.
Contract structure is the strongest predictor of churn in our data. Month-to-month customers churn at 42.7%, nearly four times the rate of one-year contracts (11.3%) and fifteen times the rate of two-year contracts (2.8%). Customers who can be moved to longer commitments early represent our highest retention opportunity.
Tenure data confirms that the first year is where we win or lose customers. Churn sits at 47.7% in months 0–12 but falls to 9.5% after 48 months. A customer who survives their first year is highly likely to stay long term — meaning early intervention has a disproportionate impact.
Add-on adoption reinforces this pattern. Customers with Online Security churn at 14.6% compared to 41.8% for those without it — a 27 percentage point gap. Add-ons increase both engagement and financial commitment, making customers significantly harder to lose.


## Combined Analysis Finding
The recommended strategy is to reduce churn at the source by transitioning high-risk customers toward longer-term contracts early in their lifecycle while increasing add-on adoption to strengthen engagement and retention.

Month-to-month customers represent 55% of the customer base yet account for 88% of total churn, making them the business’s largest and highest-leverage retention segment. Churn also concentrates heavily among customers with tenure under 12 months, who comprise just 14.7% of the base but contribute 55.4% of total churn.

While contract type is the primary driver of churn and tenure determines when churn is most likely to occur, add-ons act as a mitigating retention lever, reducing churn by approximately 21–26 percentage points across high-risk customer segments.

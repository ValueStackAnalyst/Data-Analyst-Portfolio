# E-Commerce Funnel Analysis

## Project Overview
Analysis of an e-commerce purchase funnel across four stages 
— home page, search page, payment page, and payment 
confirmation — identifying drop-off rates and conversion 
bottlenecks.

## Tools Used
- Excel (funnel calculations, drop-off rates, funnel chart)
- Tableau (funnel visualisation)

## Funnel Summary
| Stage | Users | Drop-Off Rate | Conversion Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Home page | 90,400 | — | 100% |
| Search page | 45,200 | 50.0% | 50.0% |
| Payment page | 6,030 | 86.6% | 13.4% |
| Payment confirmation | 452 | 92.5% | 7.5% |
| Overall conversion | — | — | 0.5% |

## Key Finding
> The business should immediately prioritize payment-page optimization by improving usability, transparency, and payment flexibility, as the payment stage is the funnel’s highest-friction and highest-value abandonment point. With an overall funnel conversion rate of just 0.5% — meaning only 5 in every 1,000 homepage visitors complete a purchase — even modest improvements at the payment stage could disproportionately increase revenue. Only 452 of 6,030 users completed payment, resulting in a 92.6% drop-off rate, significantly higher than the 50%–87% observed across other stages. Unlike early-stage visitors, users who reach the payment page demonstrate strong purchase intent, making them substantially more recoverable and far less costly to convert than acquiring new customers.
